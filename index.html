About Me


My current research explores how Vietnamese-English heritage speakers represent shared speech sounds (that is, "similar" sounds that exist in both language inventories) within their bilingual mind -- are these phonetic categories blended, or bordered? In particular, I'm interested in any patterns in the spectral shaping of vowels produced in both languages by switched-dominance bilinguals, and what those patterns might say about how humans acquire language.

Before coming to Northwestern, I completed my BA in Linguistics and minors in Computer Science, Psychology, and Cognitive Science from UC San Diego.
